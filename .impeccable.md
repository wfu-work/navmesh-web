## Design Context

### Users

Aegis Web is used by operations engineers, developers, and small infrastructure teams who need to monitor servers, service probes, events, assets, and security checks from a private deployment.

They usually open the product during routine inspection, incident triage, host onboarding, and risk review. The interface should help them find abnormal hosts, failed probes, unresolved events, and risky security findings quickly.

### Brand Personality

Calm, precise, reliable.

The product should feel like a serious operations console: quiet enough for repeated daily use, clear enough under pressure, and trustworthy when showing high-severity events.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a restrained operational dashboard style with light surfaces, readable tables, concise status marks, and purposeful charts. Avoid marketing-style hero layouts, decorative gradients, oversized cards, and visual noise.

Color should be functional: neutral foundations for dense data, clear severity colors for risks and events, and sparing accent color for navigation and primary actions.

### Design Principles

- Prioritize scanability over spectacle.
- Keep high-severity states visually unmistakable without making the whole UI loud.
- Place actions close to the data they affect.
- Use compact, stable layouts that support repeated operations work.
- Let charts answer operational questions: trend, current state, spike, failure, or recovery.
